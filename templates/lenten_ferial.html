In the historic Western rite, Lent alone has proper readings appointed for every weekday, not just Sundays. As a rule each day has two lessons — an Epistle (in Lent almost always from the Old Testament) and a Gospel; the Ember Days and the Wednesday and Saturday of Laetare carry extra lessons. Sunday rows are shaded, for context. Tap any reference to open it at BibleGateway. Each weekday's day card (tap the day's name) also gives the Mass's historic introit and collect — public-domain texts of the old Western rite, labelled as such since Lutheran service books never carried weekday propers (details).
